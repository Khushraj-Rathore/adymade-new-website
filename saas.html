What We Do for SaaS & Tech

The Build, Distribute, and Grow Stack for Product Teams

SaaS companies face a specific problem: the product team needs to keep shipping, the growth team needs to keep acquiring, and the company needs to stay visible in a search landscape that is increasingly driven by AI tools rather than blue links. Most agencies only solve one of these.

We work alongside SaaS teams as a flexible extension — integrating AI features into your product (without a machine learning hire), building the internal dashboards and admin tools your ops team needs, and ensuring that when a prospect asks ChatGPT or Perplexity for software recommendations in your category, your product gets cited.

We are particularly useful at the growth stage: after product-market fit, when the company needs to scale marketing and improve the product simultaneously, but the team is not yet large enough to do both well in-house at the same time.

How It Works

Scoped, Shipped, and Measured in Sprints

We work in two-week cycles so you always have working deliverables — not a six-month engagement with nothing to show at the midpoint.

1

Product & Stack Review

We review your current tech stack, your product roadmap, your marketing channels, and your growth metrics. We identify the three highest-leverage opportunities — whether in the product, the marketing, or the search visibility — and propose a sequenced programme.

2

Sprint Planning

We break the programme into two-week sprints with defined deliverables. AI integration goes in sprint 1, GEO audit and implementation in sprint 2, dashboard in sprint 3 — or in whatever sequence your current priorities demand.

3

Build & Ship

Each sprint ends with a working deliverable on your staging or live environment. You review, we refine, and the next sprint begins. No black-box development. No disappearing for weeks between check-ins.

4

Measure & Iterate

We instrument every deliverable: AI feature usage rates, GEO citation tracking, marketing CAC by channel, dashboard adoption. Data drives the next sprint's priorities. We stop what is not working and double down on what is.
