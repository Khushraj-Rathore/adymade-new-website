What Is SEO

Page 1 of Google Is Not an Accident

SEO (Search Engine Optimisation) is the process of making your website appear at the top of Google results when your target customers search for the products and services you offer. Unlike paid ads, organic rankings generate free traffic continuously — and that traffic compounds over time.

A well-executed SEO programme works on three layers simultaneously: technical health (so Google can crawl and index your site correctly), on-page quality (so Google understands what each page is about and considers it relevant), and off-page authority (so Google trusts your site over competitors).

Most websites have significant quick wins available: schema markup missing, title tags duplicate or keyword-unfocused, Core Web Vitals failing, local citations inconsistent. We find these and fix them in the first month before moving to longer-term authority building.

Key Features

SEO Done the Right Way, Not the Fast Way

Sustainable rankings require all three layers — technical foundation, on-page relevance, and off-page authority. We work all three, in the right order, for every client.

Core Web Vitals Optimisation

Google's Page Experience signals — LCP, CLS, and FID — directly affect rankings. We audit, identify, and fix the technical causes of poor scores: unoptimised images, render-blocking resources, layout shifts, and slow server responses.

Schema & Structured Data

JSON-LD schema for LocalBusiness, Service, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, Product, and Review. Structured data helps Google understand your content precisely — driving rich result features (star ratings, FAQs, sitelinks) that increase click-through rates.

Keyword Research & Mapping

We map primary, secondary, and long-tail keywords to every page on your site — aligning search intent (informational, navigational, transactional) with your content and conversion funnel. No page competes against another for the same keyword.

Authority Link Building

Outreach to relevant publications, directories, and industry sites for genuine backlinks. Guest posting on domain-authority 40+ sites. No link farms, no PBNs, no black-hat schemes — only links that survive the next Google update.

Local SEO & Google Business

Google Business Profile optimisation, local citation building (Justdial, IndiaMart, Sulekha, Yelp), review generation strategy, and local pack ranking for "[service] in [city]" searches. Critical for any business with a physical presence or local market.

Monthly Rank Tracking & Reports

Every tracked keyword's position is monitored weekly. Monthly reports show ranking movements, organic traffic changes, new keywords entering Page 1, and next month's priority actions. You always know exactly where you stand.

How It Works

Audit to Page 1 in 4 Structured Phases

SEO requires sequenced work — fixing technical issues before building links, optimising pages before chasing authority. We follow a proven sequence that produces results without shortcuts.

1

Technical Audit

Full crawl using Screaming Frog and Ahrefs to identify: crawl errors, broken links, duplicate content, missing schema, slow pages, Core Web Vitals failures, and indexation issues. You receive the full audit report, not just a summary.

2

On-Page Optimisation

Keyword mapping, title tag and meta description rewrites, heading structure, internal linking improvements, image alt text, and schema implementation. This phase typically takes 30 to 45 days for a standard site.

3

Content & Authority

Target content gaps with new blog posts and landing pages. Outreach for backlinks from relevant, authoritative sites. Local citation building for business directories. This phase runs continuously as a monthly programme.

4

Track & Report

Weekly rank position tracking across all target keywords. Monthly report with traffic and ranking changes, backlinks acquired, and priority actions for the next month. We adjust strategy based on what the data shows, not assumptions.

What We Cover

Four Pillars of Complete SEO Coverage

A ranking that lasts requires all four pillars working together. Skipping any one of them leaves gaps that competitors exploit.

1

Technical SEO

The foundation. If Google cannot crawl, index, and render your site correctly, no amount of content or links will move rankings.

  • XML sitemap creation and submission
  • Robots.txt configuration and crawl budget
  • Core Web Vitals: LCP, CLS, FID optimisation
  • HTTPS enforcement and redirect audits
  • Duplicate content and canonical tag fixes
  • Structured data / JSON-LD schema implementation
  • Mobile-first indexing readiness
  • Hreflang tags for multilingual sites (EN+AR)
2

On-Page SEO

Telling Google exactly what each page is about and why it deserves to rank — for specific, high-intent search queries.

  • Keyword research and intent mapping
  • Title tag and meta description optimisation
  • H1–H4 heading hierarchy and keyword placement
  • Content rewriting for relevance and depth
  • Internal linking strategy and anchor text
  • Image compression, alt text, and file naming
  • FAQ sections with schema markup
  • E-E-A-T signals (Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trust)
3

Link Building

Earning genuine backlinks from authoritative, relevant sites — the primary signal Google uses to judge whether your site deserves to outrank established competitors.

  • Competitor backlink gap analysis
  • Guest posting on DA 40+ industry publications
  • Business directory and citation listings
  • Broken link reclamation outreach
  • PR mentions and brand citations
  • Industry association and chamber listings
  • Monthly backlink profile health monitoring
  • Disavow file management if needed
4

Local SEO

Ranking in the Google Local Pack (maps results) and for "[service] in [city]" searches — essential for any business with a physical location or city-specific market.

  • Google Business Profile creation and optimisation
  • NAP consistency across all platforms
  • Local citation building (Justdial, Sulekha, IndiaMart)
  • Review generation and response strategy
  • Local keyword landing pages per city
  • Local schema (PostalAddress, GeoCoordinates)
  • Bing Places and Apple Maps listing
  • Local rank tracking per target city

Frequently Asked

Questions About SEO Services

Technical fixes and on-page optimisations can show ranking improvements within 4 to 8 weeks for queries where you already have some presence. New content targeting fresh keywords typically takes 3 to 6 months to rank on Page 1, depending on competition. Local SEO (Google Business Profile and local pack) tends to show results faster — often within 4 to 8 weeks for less competitive local queries. Link building takes 3 to 6 months to meaningfully impact domain authority. We set specific timelines for each keyword cluster at onboarding, based on current ranking and competition level.
No reputable SEO agency can guarantee specific rankings — Google does not disclose its algorithm, and rankings are influenced by factors outside any agency's control (competitor activity, algorithm updates, search demand changes). Any agency that guarantees a specific position by a specific date is either targeting keywords with no meaningful traffic, or they are planning to use black-hat techniques that will produce short-term results and long-term penalties. What we do commit to: targeting the right keywords for your business, executing every tactic correctly, reporting transparently, and adjusting strategy when data shows a different approach is needed.
SEO optimises your website to rank in Google's traditional search results — the list of links. GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) optimises your content so that AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews cite your business when answering questions. They serve different search behaviours and require different strategies, but they reinforce each other significantly — a strong SEO foundation (structured data, authoritative content, quality backlinks) directly improves GEO performance. We recommend running both as a combined programme for maximum search visibility, since AI and traditional search are increasingly how the same customers find the same businesses.
